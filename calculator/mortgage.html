About Mortgage Calculations

This calculator uses the standard mortgage formula to compute your monthly payment. The payment remains constant, but the proportion going toward principal vs interest changes over time.

M = P × [r(1 + r)ⁿ] / [(1 + r)ⁿ - 1]

• M = Monthly payment

• P = Principal (loan amount)

• r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12)

• n = Number of payments (years × 12)

Payment Structure

  • • Early payments are mostly interest
  • • Later payments are mostly principal
  • • Total payment stays constant
  • • More principal paid each month

Tips

  • • Shorter terms = less interest
  • • Lower rates = lower payments
  • • Extra payments reduce interest
  • • Consider PMI for down payments <20%
