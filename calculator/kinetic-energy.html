About Kinetic Energy

Kinetic energy is the energy possessed by an object due to its motion. It depends on the object's mass and velocity.

KE = ½ × m × v²

where:

• KE = Kinetic Energy (Joules)

• m = Mass (kg)

• v = Velocity (m/s)

Key Points

  • • Kinetic energy is always positive
  • • Energy is proportional to mass
  • • Energy is proportional to velocity squared
  • • Doubling velocity quadruples energy

Common Examples

  • • Walking person: ~70 J
  • • Running person: ~500 J
  • • Car at 60 km/h: ~200,000 J
  • • Bullet: ~2,000 J

Units

  • Metric: Mass in kg, Velocity in m/s → Energy in Joules (J)
  • Imperial: Mass in lbs, Velocity in mph → Energy in Foot-pounds (ft·lb)
  • 1 Joule = 0.7376 foot-pounds
  • 1 foot-pound = 1.3558 Joules

Note: Kinetic energy increases with the square of velocity, meaning small increases in speed result in large increases in energy. This is why high-speed collisions are so dangerous.
